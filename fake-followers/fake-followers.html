Audience / Follower Size

The debate around whether micro or macro influencers deliver better ROI has been ongoing. To shed light on this, we analysed 200,000 influencer campaign posts. Our findings revealed that high-performing influencers (those driving over 10 conversions per post) had a median follower count of 112K, while mid-performing influencers (3–9 conversions) averaged around 61K followers, and low-performing influencers (fewer than 3 conversions) had a median of 35K followers.

This suggests that follower size does correlate with performance, especially when surpassing the 75th percentile (approx. 95,000+ followers), where conversion rates significantly improve. However, the story doesn't end there.Micro and nano influencers, although smaller in reach, often come with lower collaboration costs and higher trust within niche communities. This makes them potentially more cost-effective, especially when budget and target relevance are factored into the ROI equation.

Verified Accounts

One intriguing factor in maximising influencer ROI is whether the influencer holds a verified badge (the blue checkmark) on Instagram. Our analysis of over 200K posts revealed that 61.1% of high-performing influencers (those generating over 10 conversions per post) had verified accounts. This dropped to 34.4% among medium performers (3–9 conversions per post) and further to 26.7% for low performers (fewer than 3 conversions per post).

While this might suggest that verified influencers drive more conversions, it's important to remember that correlation doesn’t imply causation. Verified status is often linked with larger follower bases, which may be the actual driver of better performance. So, while the checkmark can be a helpful indicator, it shouldn’t be used in isolation when selecting influencers.
